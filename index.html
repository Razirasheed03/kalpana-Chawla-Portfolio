Kalpana Chawla

About Me

Born in Karnal, India and based in the United States, I am an astronaut and aerospace engineer with NASA. With a Ph.D. in aerospace engineering, I specialize in computational fluid dynamics and have logged over 30 days in space as a mission specialist and robotic arm operator.

My journey began at Punjab Engineering College before moving to the United States for my advanced degrees at the University of Texas and University of Colorado. After joining NASA in 1988, I was selected as an astronaut candidate in 1994, fulfilling my lifelong passion for flight and space exploration.

I am dedicated to advancing our understanding of aerodynamics, space technology, and microgravity research. As one of the pioneering women in space exploration, I actively mentor aspiring scientists and engineers through educational outreach programs.

"The path from dreams to success does exist. May you have the vision to find it, the courage to get on to it, and the perseverance to follow it."

Key Projects

STS-87 Mission

November 19 - December 5, 1997

Served as mission specialist and prime robotic arm operator aboard Space Shuttle Columbia. Successfully deployed and retrieved the Spartan-201 satellite, which gathered critical data on solar wind and the sun's outer atmosphere. Conducted multiple fluid dynamics experiments in microgravity.

NASA Astronaut Corps Technical Assignments

1998 - 2002

Led technical evaluations of spacecraft systems for the Astronaut Office. Developed improved procedures for emergency response scenarios and crew training. Consulted on International Space Station robotic systems integration and operational protocols.

STS-107 Science Mission

January 16 - February 1, 2003

Served as mission specialist on this dedicated science research flight. Conducted experiments across multiple disciplines including Earth observation, advanced technology development, and biological sciences. Performed over 80 experiments operating in continuous shifts to maximize research time.

Educational Outreach Program

1995 - 2003

Developed and implemented STEM education initiatives reaching over 10,000 students in the United States and India. Created curriculum materials for K-12 space science education and mentored engineering students at multiple universities.

Selected Publications & Research

Computational Fluid Dynamics Modeling of V/STOL Aircraft

Journal of Aerospace Engineering, 1993

Groundbreaking research on vertical/short takeoff and landing aircraft aerodynamics using novel computational methods.


Wake Turbulence Analysis Using Three-Dimensional Modeling

AIAA Journal, 1995

Developed improved models for predicting aircraft wake behavior, enhancing safety protocols for commercial aviation.


Effects of Microgravity on Fluid Behavior in Spacecraft Systems

NASA Technical Report, 1998

Analysis of experimental data from STS-87 mission, resulting in design improvements for fluid management systems.


Optimization Algorithms for Robotic Arm Operations in Space

IEEE Robotics Conference, 2000

Presented innovative approaches to improving efficiency and precision of robotic operations during spacewalks and satellite deployment.
